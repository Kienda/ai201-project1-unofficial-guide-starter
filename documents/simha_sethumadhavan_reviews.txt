Essentially, if you were a big fan of Fundies, then you'll love this class. The class starts with memory systems such as DRAM, SRAM, and most importantly CACHE. A lot of memory systems can be boiled down to types of caches and their implementations within large pipelines. Out of Order processors are the next large topic that really allows you to understand the life cycle of a program. I found this part to be the most challenging with all of the micro-architecture details, but extremely cool to learn. Finally, you'll end the class talking about Network on chips, GPUs, and ML centric chips. Overall, a very interesting class. Simha makes this class manageable since it has the potential to be one of the hardest CS/EE classes. Simha is actually one of the greatest professors in the CS department, he actually spends a lot of class making sure that everybody understands what he talks about. Especially in office hours, he takes the time to get to know you as a student and help you learn. I would take any chance to go his Office hours again if given the chance.

Review content:

Sys.) and was blown away. The course was well-paced starting with the hardware fundamentals of DRAM, caches & virtual memory and gradually progressing into incorporating the software side of out-of-order processing, coherence, SIMD instructions etc. 

Prof. Sethumadhavan is a fantastic lecturer, and arguably the best out of all the CS/EE professors I've had so far. I highly recommend this class if you're a computer engineer or else if you have any mild interest in understanding how a computer actually works.
